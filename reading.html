The Orgins Of Power, Prosperity, And Poverty		 Daron Acemoglu and James A. Robinson Acemoglu and Robinson emphasize the crucial role of political institutions in shaping economic outcomes. Inclusive institutions promote broad-based participation in political and economic life, fostering innovation and prosperity. Extractive institutions, on the other hand, concentrate power and wealth in the hands of a small elite, hindering economic development.
In this book, a wide range of historical examples are drawed to support its arguments, from North and South Korea to divergent development of England and Spain.
This book give me an another way to observe a nation. And somehow it gives me a support to my intuition about the relationship between political and economic growing.
